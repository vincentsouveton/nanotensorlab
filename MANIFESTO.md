# A Manifesto

This manifesto sets out the core objectives of the project. As the field of Artificial Intelligence (AI), and more specifically Machine Learning (ML), evolve rapidly, some of these ideas may be refined, but the principles outlined here will serve as our foundation.

## AI in the Modern World

There are many ways of using AI. In the early 2020s, the field reached a major milestone with the rise of large language models and their deployment in the public sphere. Most people are now familiar with generative models capable of producing text, images, audio, and video, among other outputs. In parallel, such tools are increasingly adopted for industrial purposes. AI has become a strategic field, disrupting critical sectors from conventional industry to military applications. Recent discussions have even raised the prospect of Artificial General Intelligence and the potential obsolescence of human labor — or humanity itself. Without going that far, there are legitimate reasons to be concerned about the rapid and massive development of these technologies. As a powerful disruptive force, AI will undeniably reshape how we live, create, and work. It may also deepen our dependence on advanced systems whose inner workings remain opaque to most people.

## AI in Science

Beyond these broader societal considerations, AI has also transformed science. In a world generating ever-increasing volumes of data, ML has become an essential tool for uncovering complex relationships between variables. Today, a wide range of scientific domains have embraced the statistical learning paradigm, fundamentally changing how research is conducted. It is also reshaping the role of researchers themselves: technical execution is increasingly delegated to AI agents, while humans focus on creative and conceptual work. This paradigm will likely continue to evolve. It is plausible that, in the near future, AI agents combining creative reasoning with technical mastery will be able to tackle major scientific challenges, even in domains such as fundamental physics or mathematics.

## A Human-Centered Vision of Science

What, then, is left for human scientists? Beyond technical considerations, this project seeks to promote a vision of science inspired by the philosophy of the Enlightenment. We believe that a fundamental part of the human experience is to better understand the world we live in. We see science not as an engine for the endless development of technologies in service of capitalistic growth, but as a means to cultivate autonomy, deepen our understanding of the physical world, and enrich our experience of the universe through clear and informed observation. As sentient beings, we hold that it is essential to grow, gain mastery, and feel the weight of knowledge within us. While AI can assist in parts of the scientific process, that process remains, at its core, a human endeavor, one in which we must do everything we can to retain agency over our destiny and never lose sight of what it means to be conscious, curious beings. As students, the goal is to learn. As researchers, the goal is to answer new questions and share that knowledge with the broader public.

## What Is nanotensorlab?

In keeping with this vision, **nanotensorlab** focuses on mastering ML tools to better understand the world. It serves a dual purpose: as an **educational platform** for learning how ML models work and how they interact with data, and as a **research environment** where models can be quickly tested in the early stages of a project. By design, any project built within this framework can be scaled into a full research effort tailored to a specific scientific application. Ultimately, we see AI not as an end in itself, but as a means of **emancipation**. The world around us is woven from complex, high-dimensional data — from the structure of matter to the dynamics of living systems, from climate patterns to the signals buried in astronomical observations. By learning to decipher this complexity with the right tools and the right mindset, we reclaim the ability to understand rather than merely consume, and to grow as informed, autonomous beings navigating a fascinating world.
